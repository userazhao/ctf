Felix the Helix says:
When our cells make use of our DNA, they first have to translate it into RNA. You can think of the DNA helix as a zipper, with each base fitting like a puzzle piece into a corresponding RNA base.
  1. Adenine → Uracil
  2. Guanine → Cytosine
  3. Thymine → Adenine
  4. Cytosine → Guanine
